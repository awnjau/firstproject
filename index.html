Some Books I've Read in 2021

On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous by Ocean Vuong
4.5/5 stars
A beautifully written memoir detailing the trials and tribulations of being a gay Vietnamese man and son.
You can easily tell he's a poet. His complicated relationship with his mother and his first love is raw and emotional. With outstanding prose and many quote-worthy moments, this book will leave you questioning your own upbringing and how that shaped your identity.

A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J Mass (The First Three Books)
1.5/5 stars for the whole series
The first book, was boring and relatively uneventful. Your typical YA fantasy novel, not great not terrible. (3/5)
The second book, A Court of Mist and Fury, was bad, simply awful. Both love interests suck ass, they're possessiveness is turned into romance and I hate how mental health was villanized. Also the main character is stupid. (1/5)
The third book, A Courth of Wings and Ruin, was tiring. SJM must think herself a god to publish a book this big and this bad. It was confusing and everything about it was convoluted and unessecary. Also the performative feminism wasn't cute. (2/5)

The Lady Rogue by Jenn Bennett
3.5/5 stars
It was a cute little read to have while on vacation. Very reminiscent of Enola Holmes, which I did quite enjoy. I love the childhood friends to enemies to lovers trope, it gets me everytime. I was also really into fantasy at the time of reading it, so it definitely gave me the type of story I was looking for, but it's nothing special.

Joyland by Stephen King
4/5 stars
I actually quite liked this book. I was vitually stimulating and you could really get a sense of thie erie carnival world you've been thrust into. I thought the mystery aspect was done well, I didn't know what was going to happen so that was nice. I wasn't a big fan of the age gap relationship between the main character and that mom with the sick son, but the son was cute, definitely wierd and slightly creepy, but still cute.

The Eyes of the Dragon by Stephen King
3/5 stars
I don't know how to feel about the fact that I'm a Stephen King fan. This book was good. I don't know why I was obsessed with dragons at this time but whatever. It was a cute read, and I'm starting to realize that Steph is pretty good at giving visuals. But the mystery wasn't great; it was too obvious what the villan wanted. I hate when the chatacter is getting manipulated the whole time and they don't realize it.

We Were Liars by E Lockhart
2/5 stars
I'm no longer trusting booktok. This was shit, mostly because I had high expectations. If people didn't hype it up so much, it would've been a 2.5/5. The book is boring and all the characters suck and the twist is obvious if you've ever read "Every Last Word". I did find the portrayal of grief and survivors guilt intriging however. They could've done something.

Six of Crows by Leigh Bardugo (Duology)
4.9/5 stars
I'm sorry but this series is great. Like honest to god the best fantasy/heist books I've ever read. I usually dislike series because they get worse as they go, but Leigh managed to pull a Shrek 2 and make a sequel that's just as great as the original. The book has everything. Action. A great cast of characters. Beautifully a written relationships and arcs. And a magic system that actually makes sense (SJM should take notes). I will say though, I some of the action scenes were very hard to imagine, so there's the 0.1 star.

They Both Die at the End by Adam Silvera
3/5 stars
Not gonna lie, it was a bit boring, especially towards the middle. At first I hated the multiple perspectives from people other than the two main characters, but when I finished the book I actually really enjoyed that aspect. Though giving it a 3 might be generous, seeing how all the stories connected in the end was something kinda beautiful. I also love the speculative design and how grounded in the real world it felt. Also the couple was cute and I can appreciate what it means to have a gay love story with two men of color.

Into the Crooked Place by Alexandra Christo (Duology)
2.5/5 stars
Not a bad series nessecarily, but it felt like a bad ripoff of Six of Crows. I do like that one of the most important characters turned out to be two young black women, because that's not something you see in a lot of YA fantasy novels. And the sisterly relationship between the two of them was quite a nuanced one.

To Kill a Kingdom by Alexandra Christo
3/5 stars
I don't really understand what's so special about this book to begin with. Booktok loves it and my favorite youtuber who does book reviews also loves it, so I figured I should too. It's nice. I like the world building and I do like the characters, and I even like the romance, there was just nothing about it that I absolutely loves. The whole thing was just "good". But the writting is lyrically very whimsical, which I do like. The side chatacters are also fun.

Domincana by Angie Cruz
4/5 stars
Detailing the journey of a young Dominican girl who is sent to America to live with her creepy older husband, Domincana was a breath of fresh air for me. After being submerged in booktok reccomendations for quite some time, I was finally able to read something different. The book is written very personally, you can tell the story is close to Angie. I saw a lot of similarities between Dominican culture and mine, especially with the attatchment to the idea of an "American dream". The story did get a bit tiresome in the middle, but it picked up pace again towards the end. Overall, it was a really interesting read and I can't say I don't want to look at more of her work.

The Inheritance Games by Jennifer Lynn Barnes
4/5 stars
This was actually a surpsingly pleasant read. I expected it to be another sort of typical YA mystery and I read it as a pallet cleanser after On Earth We're Breifly Gorgeous. It was exactly what I needed at the time and the mystery was actually pretty exciting. Of course you have the whole "one girl and 4 hot guys" trope and a stereotypical love triangle, of which Jameson should be the clear choice, but there's more to that than meets the eye. I'm looking forward to reading the sequal since it ended on a fat cliff hanger.

Ninth House by Leigh Bardugo
4.5/5
Leigh Bardugo has my actual heart. The little dark academic inside of my was thriving when I read this book. Teachnically it's an Adult Novel, but it gives more NA. This is her first venture into adult writing so I understand why it feels a bit out of place in that regard. But the magic system, the main character, and the love interest (fucking Darlington) all make for an exciting supernatural novel that leaves you thinking about how irresponsible college kids would be if they had magic. It's reminiscent of VE Schwab, which is a very high compliment. I can't wait until she comes out with the sequal.

My Dark Vanessa by Kate Elizabeth Russell
4.5/5 stars
Okay this book is fucked up, full discolsure. Well, not really the book itself, that's great, but more so what happens in the book. It details the story of a 15 year old girl who gets groomed and abused by her 42 year old teacher at boarding school. In her eyes it's a romantic relationship, one where there's consent on both sides. It goes on to switch between her 15 year old self and her current 32 year old self. And throughout all that time, she still kept in contact with him, that's how deeply he affected her. I could go on a full rant about this book, but no one wants to read that so I'll just say that this book is so so so good at taking you into the mind of a young person who's being groomed. You really see how she was the perfect target and how manipulative this man was to do that to her. It's doesn't romaticize the abuse, even though Vanessa herself does. It's clear in the world of the novel that no one agrees with her when it comes to how her and this english teacher got into this situation. Don't read it if you have a weak stomach, or if these kinds of things are triggering. This was the last book I read and I have to say, I think it put me off reading for a bit because of how hard it was to get through, but it was very thoughtfully written.
